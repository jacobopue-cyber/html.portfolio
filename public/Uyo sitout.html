Uyo Composers and Performers Sit-out 2.0

Date: will be communicated.

What is this?

This isn't a board meeting or a formal seminar. The Music Creators & Performers Sitout is a relaxed hangout for composers, instrumentalists, vocalists, producers, conductors, and performers in Uyo. It's a space to connect, share ideas, showcase what we're working on, and spend time with people who understand the joys and challenges of creating and performing music.

Step 1: Arrival, Drinks & Small Chops (30 Minutes)

There is no formal start. Show up, find a comfortable spot, and order your first drink or grab some snacks. We start the vibe with full glasses, good food, and easy conversations.

What to do: Get your refreshments settled so you can relax and enjoy the rest of the evening.

Step 2: The "Aux" Session (With Refreshments)

While we are eating and sipping on our drinks, participants will take turns sharing a little bit of their work.

How it works: Connect via Bluetooth, pass the aux cable, perform a short excerpt, or play a recording.

The Vibe: Think of it as dinner music—but it's our own music. We listen, eat, and exchange casual feedback between bites.

Step 3: The 10-Minute Creative Challenge

Even while refreshments continue, we engage in a quick and fun creative exercise to spark ideas and collaboration.

The Task: A simple prompt is selected (for example, "The sound of rain in Uyo").

The Goal: Spend 10 minutes creating something inspired by the prompt—whether it's a melody, rhythm, lyric, performance concept, or musical idea. No pressure, just creativity and enjoyment.

Step 4: Music, Tech & Industry Conversations

An open discussion about the craft, tools, and opportunities available to musicians. This is also a great time to order another round and exchange insights.

Topics for Discussion

  1. Composition & Performance

    • Writing music
    • Arranging
    • Interpretation
    • Rehearsal techniques
    • Stage experiences

  2. Software & Technology

    • Comparing notation software, DAWs, virtual instruments, recording tools, and performance technology.

  3. Career Opportunities

    • Discussing gigs
    • Scoring projects
    • Teaching opportunities
    • Collaborations
    • Auditions
    • Other professional prospects

  4. Creative Challenges

    • Sharing experiences, lessons learned, and practical solutions to common musical challenges.

Step 5: Networking, Collaboration & Extra Rounds

The formal music conversations may slow down, but the sitout continues. This is the time for deeper discussions, future project planning, networking, and potential collaborations while finishing off the food and drinks.

Quick Checklist

  • Bring your phone, tablet, laptop, instrument, or any material you may want to share.
  • Bring a relaxed attitude and an openness to connect with others.
  • Come ready to learn, contribute, and enjoy yourself.
  • Everyone is responsible for their own food and drink orders unless otherwise stated.
